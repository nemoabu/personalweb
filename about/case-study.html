Case Study: Reducing Onboarding Friction

Lamina Solutions UI/UX Design Internship

Problem

The original onboarding process was fragmented. After signing up, first-time users had to:

  • Add a pet (~5 clicks)
  • Add a service (~6 clicks)
  • Add an address (~9 clicks)
Total: ~20 clicks on average to complete their profile and place an order.

This created unnecessary delays and friction in early adoption.

Solution

I designed a unified onboarding flow that integrated pet details, service selection, and address entry into one streamlined process. Additional optimizations included:

  • Surfacing address fields earlier (no hidden steps for first-time users)
  • Reducing page transitions and confirmations
  • Adding autofill and smarter defaults to cut down typing
  • Moving primary actions closer to search results to reduce pointer travel

Impact

Before ~20 clicks
After ~14 clicks
Reduction 30%

Users could complete onboarding faster and with less friction, leading to a smoother first-time experience and aligning with business goals for higher engagement.
